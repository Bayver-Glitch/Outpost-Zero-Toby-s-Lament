# OUTPOST ZERO - Setting & World Design

## PRIMARY LOCATION: PACIFIC NORTHWEST (INSPIRED BY WASHINGTON STATE)

### General Area
- **Region:** Pacific Northwest, United States
- **Terrain:** Mountain valleys, dense forests, rivers, lakes
- **Climate:** Temperate rainforest on west side, drier east side
- **Vegetation:** Douglas fir, cedar, hemlock, ferns, moss, wild berries
- **Wildlife:** Deer, elk, black bears, cougars, salmon, eagles

---

## PARADISE VALLEY (Mt. Rainier Region)

### Description
A lush valley nestled in the shadow of a massive volcanic peak. Dense evergreen forests give way to wildflower meadows in summer. A river winds through the valley floor, fed by snowmelt and mountain streams.

### In-Game Inspiration (NOT the name)
The settlement sits in a valley carved by an ancient river. Pine and fir trees blanket the hillsides. Ferns carpet the forest floor. Wild blackberries grow thick along fence lines. Deer graze in meadow clearings at dawn.

## SURROUNDING REGION

### Zones (Scavenging Areas)

#### Safe Zone (0-5 miles from camp)
- **Risk:** Low
- **Loot:** Basic supplies, food, occasional book
- **Features:** Deer, rabbits, edible plants, clean water

#### Moderate Zone (5-20 miles)
- **Risk:** Medium (zombies, raiders)
- **Loot:** Tools, weapons, books, medicine
- **Features:** Abandoned towns, old stores, gas stations

#### Danger Zone (20-50 miles)
- **Risk:** High
- **Loot:** Advanced equipment, vehicles, rare books
- **Features:** City outskirts, industrial areas, military surplus

#### Extreme Zone (50+ miles)
- **Risk:** Very High
- **Loot:** High-tech, vehicles, rare discoveries
- **Features:** Major cities, military bases, research facilities

## SEASONAL CHANGES

### Spring
- **Weather:** Rain, warming temps
- **Plants:** Salmonberries, fiddleheads, fresh greens
- **Animals:** Elk calving, deer fawning
- **Game:** Fishing season starts

### Summer
- **Weather:** Warm, dry, long days
- **Plants:** Berries everywhere, gardens grow fast
- **Animals:** Most active, hunting good
- **Crops:** Primary growing season

### Fall
- **Weather:** Rain starts, cooling
- **Plants:** Mushroom season, nuts (acorns)
- **Animals:** Fat from summer, hunting excellent
- **Game:** Deer rut, elk bugling

### Winter
- **Weather:** Snow in mountains, rain in valley
- **Plants:** Dormant, preserved foods
- **Animals:** Less active, trapping better than hunting
- **Crops:** Greenhouse only

## EXPANSION DIRECTIONS

As settlement grows, survivors can explore:

### North
- Higher elevations, colder
- Less zombie activity (cold intolerance)
- Less fertile soil
- Mountain hunting

### East
- Dryer, more plains-like
- Abandoned farms
- Wheat/corn potential
- More raiders

### South
- Lower elevation
- More zombies (more Miasma pools)
- Better farmland long-term
- Riskier but rewarding

### West
- Coast (if included)
- Ocean fishing
- Salt water
- More urban areas (higher risk)

## REGIONAL LORE (Optional)

The Pacific Northwest was hit relatively hard during the collapse. The dense population of Seattle/Tacoma meant millions became zombies. However, the mountains provided refuge. Communities formed in valleys, high ground. Some survive to this day - some friendly, some not.

The mountain valley your settlement is in... was it never heavily populated? Maybe a few cabins, a small town. That explains why it's relatively untouched compared to the cities.

**History (discoverable):**
- Day 1-7: Comet impact, confusion
- Day 8-14: First spore clouds, mass panic
- Day 15-21: Government collapses, military fails
- Day 22-30: The Change - Miasma spreads, zombies rise
- Day 31+: Survivors flee to hills, forests, mountains
- Your group arrived 2 years later, found the valley
