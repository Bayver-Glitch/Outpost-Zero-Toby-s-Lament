# OUTPOST ZERO - Game Design Document (REVISED)

## BACKSTORY

The world ended differently than anyone expected. Not with a bang, but with a whisper.

**The Collapse:**
- Global economic collapse, mass migrations, resource wars
- Governments fell one by one
- Small communities formed in the countryside
- Your group of 4 survivors has been roaming for months, scavenging to survive

**The Find:**
While scavenging an abandoned town, your group discovers a rusted-out car trunk. Inside is an old gardening book - water-damaged but readable. It's someone's old survival guide, written by a grandmotherly type who knew about growing vegetables, preserving food, and living off the land.

This changes everything. Instead of just surviving day to day... they could **build something**.

**The Decision:**
"We're done running. Let's put down roots. Let's make this work."

---

## GAME OVERVIEW

**Title:** Outpost Zero (or working title: "The Garden")

**Genre:** Settlement Building / Discovery / Survival

**Premise:**
Start with 4 tired scavengers, a gardening book, and nothing else. Build a town from nothing through exploration, discovery, and learning.

**Core Loop:**
1. Assign survivors to tasks (farming, hunting, scavenging, building)
2. Send expeditions into the wasteland to find books, supplies, survivors
3. Discover new technologies through reading and experimentation
4. Grow your settlement
5. Unlock new professions, buildings, and capabilities

**Progression:**
Stone Age → Bronze Age → Industrial Age → Modern Age

## STARTING CONDITIONS

## DISCOVERY SYSTEM

### Discovery Types:

**Books & Manuals:**
- Found in: Libraries, bookstores, schools, homes
- Each book unlocks a profession or improves existing skills
- Condition matters - water-damaged books give partial info
- Some books are in foreign languages (need translator?)

**Magazines & Newspapers:**
- Provide tips and small bonuses
- Fashion, sports, hobby magazines
- Newspapers give historical context

**Physical Discovery:**
- Tools, equipment, seeds
- Vehicles (need mechanic to fix)
- Generators (need electrician)
- Computers (need programmer)

**Experimentation:**
- Combine materials to discover recipes
- Failed experiments still teach something
- Some discoveries require prerequisites

### Discovery Tiers:

**Tier 1 (Start):**
- Basic gardening
- Fire starting
- Water purification
- Simple shelter

**Tier 2 (Early Game):**
- Farming techniques
- Animal husbandry
- Basic hunting/trapping
- Preserve food
- Basic tools/weapons

**Tier 3 (Mid Game):**
- Metalworking/blacksmithing
- Basic electricity
- Water systems
- Construction
- Basic medicine

**Tier 4 (Late Game):**
- Advanced electronics
- Internal combustion engines
- Computers
- Telecommunications
- Advanced medicine

**Tier 5 (End Game):**
- Robotics
- Automation
- Advanced energy (solar, nuclear)
- Terraforming

## GAME MECHANICS

### Survivor Stats:
- Health
- Stamina
- Skills (varies by profession)
- Knowledge points (to learn from books)
- Personality traits

### Day Cycle:
Each "day" = ~2 minutes real-time
- Morning: Assign tasks
- Midday: Tasks execute
- Evening: Results, events
- Night: Rest, recovery, planning

### Task System:
- Each survivor gets 1 task per day
- Some tasks take multiple days (building, research)
- Tasks have success rates based on skill
- Failures can lead to injuries, lost materials

### Scavenging Runs:
Send 1-4 survivors into the wasteland:
- Risk: Zombies, raiders, hazards
- Reward: Books, supplies, survivors, vehicles
- Time limit: Must return before dark or face night dangers
- Map reveals as scouts level up

### Wasteland Zones:
| Zone Type | Danger | Loot Quality |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| Residential | Low | Books, tools, food |
| Commercial | Medium | Electronics, supplies |
| Industrial | High | Machinery, fuel |
| Military | Very High | Weapons, ammo |
| Research | Very High | Tech, experiments |
| Abandoned Vehicles | Medium | Parts, fuel |

### Events:
- Weather changes
- Survivor arrivals (want to join)
- Raider attacks
- Animal attacks
- Disease outbreaks
- Lucky finds

## UNIQUE MECHANICS

**The Knowledge System:**
- Reading isn't instant - takes time
- Comprehension checks (intelligence/stat based)
- Partial understanding possible
- Books can be damaged/incomplete

**The Scavenging:**
- Limited "safe" trips early on
- Map reveals progressively
- Can't be everywhere at once
- Choices matter

**Professions Level Up:**
- Do the job = gain XP
- Better equipment = faster leveling
- Teachers speed up training

**No "Game Over":**
- Just setbacks
- Lose survivors? Rebuild.
- Lose supplies? Scavenge more.
- The goal is long-term growth
